Greek Mythology: The ancient Greeks had a pantheon of gods and goddesses, including Zeus, Hera, Athena, Apollo, and many others.

Roman Mythology: Roman mythology was heavily influenced by Greek mythology, and it featured deities like Jupiter, Juno, Mars, and Venus.

Norse Mythology: Norse mythology from Scandinavia included gods like Odin, Thor, Freyja, and Loki.

Hinduism: Hinduism has a vast and diverse pantheon of gods and goddesses, including Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Lakshmi, Saraswati, and many more.

Egyptian Mythology: The ancient Egyptians had a pantheon that included Ra, Isis, Osiris, and Anubis, among others.

Celtic Mythology: The Celts had a variety of deities, including Lugh, Brigid, and Morrigan.

Chinese Mythology: Chinese mythology features deities like Jade Emperor, Guan Yu, and Quan Yin.

Japanese Shinto: Shintoism involves the worship of kami, which can be deities, spirits, or ancestors.

Aztec Mythology: The Aztecs had a complex pantheon that included gods like Huitzilopochtli and Quetzalcoatl.

African Traditional Religions: Various African cultures have their own pantheons and spirits, such as the Yoruba gods in the Yoruba religion.

Native American Religions: Indigenous cultures in the Americas often have pantheons of spirits and deities unique to their traditions.

Sumerian Mythology: Ancient Sumer featured gods like Anu, Enlil, and Inanna.

Inca Mythology: The Incas had gods such as Inti, the sun god.

Buddhism: While Buddhism does not focus on a pantheon of gods, some forms of Buddhism incorporate deities and celestial beings.

Jainism: Jainism does not have a pantheon of gods but emphasizes spiritual development and enlightenment.
